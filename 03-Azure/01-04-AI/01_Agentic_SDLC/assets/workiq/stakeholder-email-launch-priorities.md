**Subject:** Launch week — what I need us focused on

Team,

We're in the final stretch and I want everyone pointed at the same handful of things. Not an exhaustive list — the "if these slip, launch feels broken" list.

In rough priority order:

1. **Checkout has to let people actually buy.** Getting from a full cart to a paid, confirmed order is the whole ballgame. This is the top of the list — everything else is behind it.

2. **Marketing is counting on discount codes for launch week.** The entire campaign is built around an offer, and we've told customers — including at least one big branch account Dan closed — that they'll be able to use a code when they buy. Right now the product has no way for a customer to do that, and support is already getting asked. I don't want launch day to be the moment we discover we can't honour what marketing promised. Someone needs to take this from "everyone agrees we need it" to a properly shaped, testable piece of work — please pull the context together (the launch channel and the support queue both have plenty) rather than guessing at the details.

3. **Customers keep asking to see their past orders.** I know this is already planned — I'm not adding it, just reinforcing it matters for the repeat-order accounts.

4. **Product search** would take a lot of friction out of large orders. Also, I believe, already on the backlog. Lower than the above for launch but keep it warm.

Let's not gold-plate any of these. I'd rather have the top two genuinely solid than four things half-done. Priya's going to help shape the newer asks into something the team can actually pick up — if it's not clearly scoped with a way to test it, it's not ready.

Thanks — this is going to be a good one.

Elena
